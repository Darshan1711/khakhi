About

An online learning platform is a digital space that allows course creators to market, sell, and deliver their eLearning courses. They are often referred to as “online course marketplaces.” Creative ideas are very important. Like traditional schools, such platforms offer learners a safe environment in which to learn, access course materials, and, in many cases, interact with both teachers and students alike. They offer a wide range of features, including, but not limited to lectures, assignments, quizzes, learning interactions, completion certificates, and social forums/chats.

As mentioned, an online learning platform is more of a marketplace. It focuses on the perspective of learners or “buyers.” That is because online learning platforms do much of the heavy lifting when it comes to marketing your courses, growing your audience, and building your sales funnel. So here, the priority is the front-end part of your business the course marketplace, as well as the courses themselves.

In contrast, online course platforms focus more on the back-end portion of things. They often involve instructor-focused, cloud-based software that enables you to not only create courses, but effective learning environments. For example, you can upload various types of learning materials and arrange them into learning paths.

As for learning management systems (LMSs), they are less focused on sales and marketing and more focused on seamlessly delivering training to large groups of learners, employees, or partners, for example. Compared with online educational learning platforms, many LMSs have built-in course authoring capabilities, enabling you to create new online courses. Besides, LMSs allow you to manage users, assign content to them, and track learner progress all under one roof. .
